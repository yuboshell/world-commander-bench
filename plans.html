Each plan follows the same skeleton so they can be compared and criticized: objective (the question), a falsifiable hypothesis, the approach, the experimental design (environment, baselines, metrics), milestones, risks with de-risking, and the target venue. Plans are living documents: the P2 plan should be sanity-checked with Enmao (efficient ML), P4 with Hongsong (RL/MARL), and P5 with the motion-generation side before work starts.

P1 — Real-time command benchmark (active — Leaf 1)

Objective. Measure what real-time command costs: macro command-following fidelity as a function of the decision deadline and the VRAM budget, in StarCraft II with the clock unpaused.

  • Hypothesis. Fidelity degrades sharply once the deadline crosses the model's decision latency (already observed as the drop-late frontier, ~87% → ~62% around the ~25 s latency), and the degradation ordering across models and budgets is not predicted by static-text benchmarks.
  • Approach. LLM-PySC2 harness, hard drop-late clock (validated), a scripted commander issuing a fixed macro strategy (build orders, expansions, tech switches, composition targets); executors are off-the-shelf and pruned LLMs (1B–70B), prompted — no training in this project.
  • Experimental design. The core engineering is the order schema: each macro order maps to an observable game-state predicate (built X by t? expanded on cue? composition within tolerance?), so fidelity is scored mechanically per order. Sweep (deadline × VRAM × model scale); hundreds of orders per point (per-order scoring gives far larger n than per-game win rate, the 06-21 lesson). Memory probes: orders whose execution depends on information observed minutes earlier (a scouted tech switch) — the eviction stress test. Win rate reported only as context.
  • Milestones. (1) Order schema + fidelity scorer; (2) frontier sweep at 2–3 model scales; (3) memory-probe suite; (4) public harness + report.
  • Risks / de-risking. Metric gaming (orders followed while the game collapses) — pair fidelity with a game-state sanity band; auto-attack leakage — keep the schema strictly macro; scoring ambiguity — freeze the predicate definitions before the sweep and publish them.
  • Venue. NeurIPS Datasets & Benchmarks or ICLR (benchmark + analysis). Machines: yubopc (SC2) + amax (model serving).

P2 — Efficiency methods in-game (next — Milestone 2)

Objective. Determine which inference-efficiency method preserves execution fidelity most cheaply inside the live game loop.

  • Hypothesis. The method ranking established on static text (perplexity, LongBench-style recall) re-ranks under the live clock: game-state streams violate the heavy-hitter/attention-sink assumptions, and one evicted standing order is catastrophic where one dropped token in prose is benign.
  • Approach. Port KV-cache eviction (StreamingLLM sinks, H2O, SnapKV, OBCache), quantization, and one lossless accelerator (speculative decoding / speculative actions) into the P1 harness; compare at equal (latency, VRAM). The method contribution: a game-aware eviction variant that pins entity state and standing orders (exploiting entity lifetimes and spatial locality) instead of attention statistics.
  • Experimental design. One variable at a time (fix VRAM, sweep deadline; then the reverse); P1's fidelity + memory probes as the score; a failure taxonomy — which class of order gets forgotten by which method — as the analysis payload.
  • Milestones. (1) One method end-to-end (= the program's Milestone 2); (2) the full sweep; (3) game-aware eviction prototype vs the ported baselines.
  • Risks / de-risking. Serving-stack engineering (KV hooks in vLLM) — start with the method whose reference code is closest to vLLM; latency–memory confounds — the one-variable protocol above.
  • Venue. ICML / NeurIPS (efficient inference). Lead fit: Enmao's field; Yubo owns the harness.

P3 — Game-state tokenizer (planned)

Objective. Cut tokens per decision at equal command-following fidelity by replacing text serialization of game state with a learned discrete code.

  • Hypothesis. A learned tokenizer over structured state (entities, positions, event streams) reaches several-fold fewer tokens per decision than text serialization at equal fidelity, and the saving compounds: fewer tokens → lower latency and smaller KV cache.
  • Approach. Graph tokenization (the Graph Tokenization ICLR 2026 line) over StarCraft II replay states; delta/stream encoding for successive states (the Δ-IRIS observation); a small adapter so the executor LLM reads the codes (this project does include light training, unlike P1).
  • Experimental design. Corpus from SC2 replays; drop-in comparison inside the P1 harness — text serialization vs learned codes at matched budgets; measure tokens/decision, end-to-end latency, fidelity, plus state-reconstruction quality as the tokenizer's own ceiling check.
  • Milestones. (1) Replay corpus + state schema; (2) tokenizer v0 (graph → codes, reconstruction validated); (3) harness integration and the frontier comparison.
  • Risks / de-risking. The LLM may not exploit learned codes without adaptation — budget for the adapter from the start; map/patch drift — hold out maps; losing human readability — keep a code→text decoder for debugging.
  • Venue. ICLR / NeurIPS (representation + efficiency). Lead: Enmao; depends on the P1 harness.

P4 — Command→coordination (dormant bud)

Objective. Turn one order into many agents' goals with a learned policy that scales past rule-based assignment.

  • Hypothesis. Rule-based assignment satisfies concrete orders at small N but degrades with crowd size and order abstraction ("hold the line" has no per-unit rule); a MARL policy conditioned on the interpreted order closes that gap at bounded compute.
  • Approach. First quantify the gap: rule-based assignment in the arena and SC2 squad scenarios, scored by per-order satisfaction as N grows. Then a learned layer (MAPPO/QMIX-family), reward = command satisfaction + cost penalties, curriculum from small N; the interpreter's structured intent is the policy's conditioning input.
  • Experimental design. Order satisfaction vs N (rules vs learned); coordination quality (collisions, coverage, formation error); training and inference compute reported — the budget axis stays.
  • Milestones. (1) Rule baseline + gap curve; (2) small-N MARL matching rules; (3) the scale study where rules break and the policy holds.
  • Risks / de-risking. The O(N²) interaction simulation (known bottleneck) — neighbour pruning / spatial hashing before any learning; reward hacking on abstract orders — start with concrete orders, add reward-from-language or preference data only when needed.
  • Venue. AAMAS or a NeurIPS/ICLR MARL track. Lead: Hongsong. Dormant until P1 exposes the coordination gap in data.

P5 — Crowd-motion executor (parked — Phase 3)

Objective. Language-commanded, full-body crowds generated in real time on one consumer GPU. (Condensed from the detailed protocol in the docs repo, plan/research-plan.md.)

  • Hypothesis. A per-character real-time generator (the MotionLCM/MotionPCM class) plus a coordination layer can hold command satisfaction and motion realism while meeting an FPS/VRAM budget that scales to tens of characters — the budget-frontier curve is the contribution (the 0/81 corner of the survey).
  • Approach. Interpreter → coordination layer → per-character real-time generator; swap the current offline model (OmniControl, ~16× too slow) for a real-time one; synthesize the missing command→motion corpus (sim → LLM paraphrase → render) with a small human-issued set as the honest test.
  • Experimental design. Command satisfaction (concrete orders scored mechanically; abstract ones human-rated), realism (FID on HumanML3D protocol), and the budget frontier (FPS, latency, VRAM vs character count); baselines: CrowdMoGen (offline) and independent per-character generation (no coordination).
  • Milestones. (1) Single character, real-time swap validated; (2) small crowd + coordination layer; (3) the budget-frontier study.
  • Risks / de-risking. Synthetic→real phrasing gap — test on the human set and measure the drop; abstract-intent grounding (the genuinely open part) — classify-then-act or reward-from-language, not supervision; crowd realism — the coordination layer plus collision/formation metrics.
  • Venue. SIGGRAPH / CVPR (motion generation & graphics). Parked until Phase 3; Motion BPE below is its on-ramp.

Motion BPE (standalone parallel project)

Objective. Establish what BPE buys over raw VQ motion tokens: reversible, training-free, variable-length motifs — and a measured latency/memory win for generation.

  • Hypothesis. BPE over discretized motion (v0 probe: compression to 0.13× vs 0.43× for a shuffled control; motifs up to 32 frames) yields ~7× fewer autoregressive steps per second of generated motion at equal quality — a budget win no fixed-length VQ pipeline (MotionGPT, T2M-GPT) provides.
  • Approach. (a) Replace the k-means stand-in with a trained VQ-VAE and verify round-trip reconstruction — the quality ceiling for everything downstream; (b) train a small autoregressive generator on motif tokens; (c) strengthen the probe's control with an AR(1)/Markov surrogate to separate true recurrence from temporal smoothness.
  • Experimental design. The key ablation: BPE-motif tokens vs raw VQ tokens, same generator, same compute budget — FID / R-precision on the HumanML3D protocol at matched steps and latency, plus tokens-per-second-of-motion. Single character only; crowd is out of scope.
  • Milestones. (1) VQ-VAE + round-trip check; (2) generator + the BPE-vs-VQ ablation; (3) the differentiator analysis vs MotionGPT (reversibility, interpretability of motifs, budget).
  • Risks / de-risking. Reconstruction ceiling too low — residual VQ (the MoMask recipe) as the upgrade path; motif reuse too sparse on small data — scale sequences/subjects before concluding; "BPE gain washes out after training" — that is exactly what the equal-budget ablation decides, and a null result is still publishable as an analysis.
  • Venue. Motion-generation community (CVPR / ECCV / SIGGRAPH Asia). Merges with the program only at Phase-3 embodiment.
