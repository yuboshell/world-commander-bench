World Commander — StarCraft II testbed

Updated: 2026-06-21 · Machine: yubopc (RTX 4060, 8 GB) · Game: SC2 5.0.15 · Harness: LLM-PySC2 (patched) · Model: Qwen3-4B-AWQ via vLLM · members-only

TL;DR
  • Capability — on the balanced 3s5z matchup the 4B only matches the game's built-in auto-attack (≈77% vs ≈67% win rate, +10 pp, not significant).
  • Clock — it takes ~27 s per decision, about 25× too slow to act in real time. The binding constraint is latency, not capability.
  • Contribution — a validated drop-late primitive (a true real-time clock that discards late decisions) — the reusable piece the program needs.

  • Harness = LLM-PySC2: the software between model and game. It turns the live game into a text description, sends it to the model, parses the reply back into in-game clicks, and advances the game.
  • Command vocabulary (a small fixed "language" the model writes): <Attack_Unit(tag)>, <Move_Screen([x,y])>, <Stop()>, and two unit abilities — <Blink([x,y])> (a Stalker's short teleport, to reposition or escape) and <Charge()> (a Zealot's rush to close in).
  • The two unit types in 3s5z: Stalkers (ranged) and Zealots (melee), 3 + 5 per side.

③ Trading in the melee

④ Losing — last unit retreats

"Our last Stalker is at 3% health and critically damaged — Blink away to survive."

<Blink(0x.., [81,45])> <Move_Screen([81,45])>

The reasoning is sensible RTS micro — focus the nearest/weakest enemy, use abilities — but "attack the nearest" is largely what units already do on their own, which is why the model's edge over auto-attack is small (Section 4).

4 · Results

Capability — does the LLM beat the game's own AI?

SC2 units auto-attack on their own, so a win alone proves nothing. Controlling against a no-LLM baseline and pooling the high-n runs:

conditionwin raten
LLM in the loop49/64 ≈ 77%64
no effective LLM (auto-attack)32/48 ≈ 67%48
  • Difference +10 pp, p ≈ 0.25 — not significant; the gap shrank as n grew (+22 → +17 → +10).
  • Win rate tracks the matchup: overwhelming maps auto-win, unwinnable maps auto-lose, and only the balanced 3s5z leaves any room — where the 4B adds a small, noisy lift.

The clock — latency is the wall

  • ~27 s / decision (range 25–30 s), prefill-dominated — the prompt is 2.4–5.7 k tokens (the full unit-by-unit state); output is only ~150–315 tokens.
  • So the 4B is ~25× too slow for second-scale control. The lever for real-time is a shorter prompt + KV-cache reuse, not just a smaller model.
  • drop-late (our real-time clock) discards a decision that misses its deadline. Validated: at a 10 s deadline, 13/13 late replies dropped, attacks fell 45 → 1, and the agent reverts to auto-attack.

5 · Methods & caveats

  • Win/loss read from the obs-list-episode<N>-{win,lose,tie}.pkl suffix; auto-attack confound controlled with no-LLM runs (WCB_SC2_MAX_QUERIES=0).
  • The demo is rendered top-down from per-step unit data (scripts/render_sc2_demo.py), so it is camera-independent — every unit is always shown.
  • Single model (4B); 8 GB GPU caps size. Binomial CIs are wide (pooled n ≈ 48–64/side).
  • Full data, code patch, and the analysis trail: results/sc2/sweep_2s3z_4B.md in the repo.
