Embodiment (E3) — commanding a body under a clock

A third World Commander environment: a character at a desk must press a button before it goes dark. It adds the axis E1/E2 lack — physical execution time — because acting is a real, timed motion.

How the experiment works

  1. A button lights at random. One button on the panel turns on for a window W and must be pressed before it goes dark; then all dark, then another, and so on.
  2. The commander reasons and issues an order. In deployment a human watches the panel and commands. For these automated runs an LLM commander stands in for the human: it views the world state, reasons, and issues a natural-language order referring to the lit button.
  3. The executor grounds the order and acts. A second LLM — the executor — reads the order plus the state, resolves which button it means, and the character reaches to press it (a timed reach: distance / hand speed).
  4. Success = the executor grounded the right button and executor processing time + reach time ≤ W.

What we measure (the focus): the executor's processing time — grounding the order. In deployment the human is the commander, so the system's real job is to turn each order into the right action fast enough to beat the clock. The commander's time is reported separately — it stands in for the human and is not on the system's critical path.

At short windows the reach (not the LLM) binds — success climbs to the grounding ceiling as W grows. The E3 analog of the arena's deadline frontier, now with an embodied execution term. L0 is pure-Python (a reach-time model, no renderer); higher fidelity (2D arm; a text-to-motion model as the fast layer) is future work. The executor's grounding latency is small here because the prompt is tiny and the output is one word — consistent with the arena finding that output length, not input context, dominates latency.
